Why

13L / 31R is closed until winter 2020, and historically has handled 1/3 of all arrivals into JFK.

When the wind results in a ~30 knot crosswind or greater on 4L/22R and 4R/22L, JFK is forced to use only a single runway (13R/31L) for all arrivals and departures.

How is this calculated?

Weather forecast data comes from Dark Sky and provides 7 days of forecasted wind gusts and direction.

Runways 22/4 are oriented 220° and 40° from magnetic north, so for all winds not exactly at 220° or 40°, there is some component that represents a crosswind.

Winds at 310° and 130° represent perfect crosswinds, so their gust strength equals crosswind strength. For all other windbearings, taking the cosine of the deviation from the perfect crosswind and multipling by the magnitude of the wind gust gives the crosswind component.

Note that wind direction (true) is converted to magnetic before calculating cross-wind relative to runways (magnetic).
